    EEG measures summated activity # Neurons communicate through a combination of chemical neurotransmitters and electrical gradients, and electroencephalography, or EEG, detects those electrical gradients to provide insight into the activity of the brain. Realize, however, that any single neuron’s electrical activity is far too miniscule to be detected by scalp EEG, and thus what we see on EEG is actually a summation of many neurons’ activity; in fact, we require at least 6 square centimeters of synchronized cortical activity for anything to be detected on scalp EEG. ...
